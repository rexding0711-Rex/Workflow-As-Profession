# Information Search Guide

Obtain detailed information through a "two-phase" search approach:
1. Broad information search: Use search tools to obtain a wide range of search results;
  * This phase aims to provide broad background information and filter out valuable sources for in-depth investigation. After initial collection is complete, organize the results from the initial collection phase, restructuring and splitting the information ("Information -> Topic Clusters"). From the results filtered during the broad search phase, select the most valuable sources and extract potential sub-directions (e.g., technology trends, investment and financing, regulatory policies, failure cases)
  * Summarize and organize your initial search results, inform the user of the key areas and directions you consider worthy of in-depth research, then proceed to deep research
2. In-depth information research: For content that needs deeper exploration within each sub-direction, use search tools to obtain more relevant information; you may use browser-related tools to thoroughly review URLs you consider useful.
  * For Excel files, use Python for precise data extraction and analysis to obtain more intuitive conclusions
  * After completing in-depth research across all directions, summarize key data, references, core findings, and information gaps; comprehensively organize all content, distilling cross-direction common insights, comparative analyses, and narrative logic; ensure each statement expresses non-redundant content, presenting viewpoints from multiple angles and formats (text + numbers)
  * If new questions arise during the organization process, or if you feel the understanding of a certain sub-direction is insufficient, supplementary searches may be conducted. Use rolling research to ensure content completeness
